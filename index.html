Overview

The Material Balance Equation (MBE) is a fundamental reservoir engineering tool used to estimate Original Oil In Place (OOIP) and understand reservoir drive mechanisms. It is derived from the law of conservation of mass.

This calculator implements the general Havlena-Odeh form of the MBE, which accounts for oil expansion, gas cap expansion, connate water and rock compressibility, and water influx simultaneously.

Use the preset scenarios in the sidebar to quickly load common reservoir configurations and see how each drive mechanism affects the result.

The General Material Balance Equation

The equation solved for N (OOIP) — exactly as derived from the PDF:

N = [Np·(Bo + (Rp−Rs)·Bg) − (We − Wp·Bw) − Ginj·Bginj − Winj·Bwinj]
/ [(Bo−Boi) + (Rsi−Rs)·Bg + m·Boi·(Bg/Bgi−1) + Boi·(1+m)·((Swi·cw+cf)/(1−Swi))·Δp]

Note: Bt = Bo + (Rsi−Rs)·Bg is computed internally. The denominator terms represent: (1) dissolved gas + oil expansion, (2) gas cap expansion, (3) connate water and rock expansion.

Drive Indices — With A = Np[Bt + (Rp−Rsi)·Bg]:
DDI = N(Bt−Bti)/A  |  SDI = N·m·Bti·(Bg/Bgi−1)/A  |  WDI = (We−WpBw)/A  |  EDI = N·Boi(1+m)[(cwSwi+cf)/(1−Swi)]·Δp/A
DDI + SDI + WDI + EDI = 1.0

Preset Scenarios

Volumetric Reservoir: No water influx (We = 0) and no gas cap (m = 0). Depletion drive only. Oil expansion and compressibility are the sole drive mechanisms.

Water Drive: Active aquifer support. We is significant relative to production. Gas cap is absent or negligible. Common in edge-water and bottom-water reservoirs.

Gas Cap Drive: Gas cap present (m > 0) with or without water influx. Gas expansion is the primary or secondary drive. Gas cap shrinks as pressure declines.

Custom: All parameters configurable. Use this to model complex reservoirs with multiple simultaneous drive mechanisms.

Tips & Common Warnings

Negative N result: Indicates inconsistency in inputs — often caused by Rp < Rsi, incorrect Bt values, or We much larger than withdrawals.

Division by zero: The denominator approaches zero when all expansion terms cancel. Check that Bt > Bi and that at least one drive mechanism is active.

R_p < R_si warning: Producing GOR below initial solution GOR is physically unusual in depletion. Verify your pressure data and PVT tables.

Swi range: Connate water saturation should be between 0.10 and 0.40 for most conventional reservoirs. Values outside this range trigger a warning.

Always cross-check MBE results against volumetric estimates. Large discrepancies may indicate undetected aquifer support or compartmentalization.

Methodology

Based on the general MBE as presented in reservoir engineering literature. Accounts for:

  • Oil and dissolved gas expansion
  • Free gas cap expansion
  • Connate water expansion
  • Rock pore volume compressibility
  • Natural water influx
  • Produced water withdrawal
